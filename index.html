About

I am an Electronics Engineer from Universidad Distrital (Bogotá), and currently an international student in France at IMT Atlantique, I am trilingual, fluent in English, French, and Spanish, and comfortable working in international and multicultural environments, training to become a generalist engineer with a strong specialization in wireless communications and embedded systems.

My academic and project experience combines hardware design, system simulation, and real-world implementation. I have worked on control systems analysis using MATLAB/Simulink, electronic circuit design and PCB development (Altium, Proteus, Multisim), embedded firmware development on ARM Cortex-M platforms, and RTOS-based architectures.

I am particularly interested in intelligent systems at the edge — deploying AI models such as CNNs and computer vision pipelines (YOLO) using PyTorch, TensorFlow, and TensorFlow Lite Micro. I have explored model optimization, quantization, and on-device inference using platforms like Edge Impulse.

My interests also extend to IoT architectures and telecommunications, including TCP/IP and OSI layers, RPL routing, 4G/5G protocol fundamentals, PDU session analysis, and secure communication design. I have studied cybersecurity concepts applied to IoT systems, including AES encryption, hash functions, authentication, and secure firmware principles.

Beyond software and electronics, I have experience in automation fundamentals, PLC concepts, power system simulation (Factory I/O, Automation Studio), and mechanical design using SolidWorks and Fusion 360.

I am motivated by interdisciplinary engineering challenges where hardware, software, communication systems, and artificial intelligence converge to create practical, efficient, and scalable solutions.
